Long Article (Progress Indicator)

This section contains extended text to test the progress indicator and read-time estimation. When the progress indicator is enabled, you should see a bar at the top of the page filling as you scroll, along with an estimated reading time in the top-right corner.

The human brain processes visual information at remarkable speed, but sustained attention requires considerable cognitive effort. For individuals with ADHD, managing this cognitive load while reading online content presents unique challenges. Distracting animations, auto-playing media, and cluttered layouts all compete for attention, making it difficult to focus on the primary content.

Research has shown that reducing visual noise, increasing text readability, and providing progress feedback can significantly improve reading comprehension and focus. Tools like reading rulers help guide the eye along individual lines of text, while font adjustments can make content more accessible for those with dyslexia or other reading difficulties.

Text-to-speech technology adds another dimension to accessibility. By combining visual highlighting with auditory feedback, readers can maintain their place more easily and engage with content through multiple sensory channels. The Web Speech API makes this possible directly in the browser without any external services.

Color adjustments serve a dual purpose: reducing eye strain during extended reading sessions and improving contrast for better readability. Warm filters reduce blue light exposure, while high-contrast modes can help text stand out against backgrounds that might otherwise cause reading fatigue.

The combination of these tools creates a comprehensive reading environment that adapts to individual needs. Each feature can be toggled independently and configured per-site, recognizing that different websites may require different levels of accommodation.

Progress indicators provide a sense of accomplishment and help with time management, both of which are important for individuals who may struggle with task persistence. Knowing how much of an article remains can help maintain motivation to continue reading.

Motion reduction is perhaps the most immediately impactful feature for ADHD users. Animated advertisements, spinning loading indicators, and auto-playing videos are all potent attention magnets that pull focus away from the content a user is trying to read. By pausing these distractions, the reading environment becomes significantly calmer.
